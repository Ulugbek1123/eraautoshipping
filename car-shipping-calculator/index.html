    6 Factors That Impact On Car Shipping Cost

    The Nexus car shipping cost calculator tells you how much it will cost to ship a car. Our calculator uses extenuating automobile transport factors to determine your overall transport cost. Here are all of the variables that our online calculator uses to provide the most accurate car transport quotes to you:

    • Distance
    • Location
    • Make & Model
    • Vehicle Condition
    • Expedited Car Transport
    • Time of Year
    • Your Price

    Distance

    The longer the route, the lower your automobile shipping price per mile. However, longer distances will increase the total cost of transporting a car with a professional car transport company. Long distance shipping on a safe carrier can be affordable, though, if you pick the right auto transport provider.

    Location

    Metropolitan areas tend to have the most drivers available and the lowest car shipping quotes, while rural towns usually cost more. If you live in a smaller town, you can keep costs down by arranging for a more urban pickup destination and driving the rest of the way in your vehicle to meet your car carrier.

    Make & Model

    Your vehicle type will undoubtedly affect your overall enclosed or open transport cost. The bigger the vehicle, the more space it takes up on the trailer (and the higher the car shipping cost climbs.) Bike racks and other accessories can change your vehicle's dimensions and increase the cost of open carrier and closed carrier services.

    Vehicle Condition

    An inoperable car requires additional labor to winch or forklift onto and off the trailer at pickup and delivery, raising the shipping cost. Most car transport companies need your vehicle's condition to be good enough to at least brake, steer, and roll onto a trailer.

    Expedited Car Transport

    Choosing an exact pickup day requires careful planning by your vehicle shipping company. That will increase the cost of shipping. If you’re running out of time and need to ship right away with a car transport service, expedited vehicle shipping is for you. The extra expense of expedited open or enclosed transport is offset by timely, efficient car transport services.

    Time of Year

    Seasonal availability is one of the driving factors behind car shipping costs in the auto transport industry. When fewer auto transport carriers are on the road and fewer vehicle shipments are rolling through your preferred pickup and delivery points, automobile shipping costs may increase.

    How Much Does it Cost to Ship a Car in Different Seasons?

    Shipping a Car in Winter

    Shipping a car in the winter can take longer than in other seasons because snow and ice can cause delays. Also, the drivers working for your auto transport company have less daylight to work. As the days get shorter and the conditions get tougher for car haulers to transport cars, things can get tricky. However, there are some choice times for you to consider when looking for a cheap automobile shipping quote and a carrier you can trust.

    December – The holiday season means drivers take time off to spend with their families, so try to book with auto transporters three to four weeks in advance.

    January – Many people migrate south to warmer states during this time. Your auto shipper may increase vehicle shipping rates due to increased demand for car shipping service.

    February – One of the slower months for the entire industry. Car haulers return to the road from holiday vacations. But, demand for car carrier services is still low. Vehicle shipment rates tend to be the cheapest during this month.

    Shipping a Car in Spring

    Early springtime is one of the best seasons to ship your car with a professional car transport service. The weather conditions improve dramatically, the days get longer, and more car transport drivers are on the road and ready to ship a car for you.

    March – Vehicle shipment rates remain low with most auto transport providers until the end of the month when people move back north in large numbers to enjoy the warmer months.

    April and May – There can be a backlog when shipping cars from southern to northern states due to high demand, increasing car transportation rates for open carrier and closed carrier services.

    Shipping a Car in Summer

    The summer months are the peak season for car transportation. Whether you need an open carrier or a closed carrier, demand is high for the services of car transport companies. Universities are letting out, employees are relocating, and auto transporters are swamped by people taking their vacations. Fortunately for vehicle shipping company customers, increased competition in the auto transport industry drives vehicle transport prices down.

    June, July and August – All three months are equally busy, with demand and driver availability for enclosed and open car transport at their highest points. This makes for little monthly difference in car shipping costs, whether you’re transporting a car across the country or simply having a vehicle shipped across state lines.

    Shipping a Car in Fall

    Milder weather and more driver availability than in the summer months lead to a slightly lower car shipping cost when you transport your vehicle. While it may not offer the same car transport discounts as the late winter, it's still a good time to contact an auto transport provider when you're in the market to ship a vehicle.

    September – An increase in college students shipping their cars may increase vehicle shipping prices slightly when you ship your vehicle.

    October and November – Slower months with good auto transporter availability and cheap vehicle shipping rates. You can even schedule an enclosed trailer for a lower price with most car shippers.
